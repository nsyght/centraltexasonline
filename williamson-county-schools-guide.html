Williamson County Schools Guide: Round Rock ISD, Georgetown ISD, Leander ISD

📅 April 2026  ·  CentralTexas.online Editorial

School district quality is one of the most important factors in home-buying decisions in Williamson County — and for good reason. The county's school districts vary significantly in resources, performance, and growth trajectory. Here's the practical guide for families navigating this decision.

The Major Williamson County School Districts

🏫 Round Rock ISD

Round Rock ISD is one of the largest and most established school districts in Central Texas, serving approximately 50,000+ students across Round Rock, Pflugerville, and portions of Cedar Park and Hutto. The district consistently earns strong state accountability ratings and has a robust advanced academics program including dual enrollment and AP courses. The district's size means significant resources for programs, facilities, and athletics. High school campuses include Round Rock High, Cedar Ridge, McNeil, and Stony Point. RRISD's dual-language program is among the strongest in the region.

🏫 Leander ISD

Leander ISD serves Cedar Park, Leander, Liberty Hill, and surrounding communities and has been one of the fastest-growing school districts in Texas. The district consistently earns "Exemplary" or "A" ratings from the state and has a strong reputation for both academics and athletics. The rapid growth has required ongoing new campus construction — LISD has opened multiple new elementary, middle, and high school campuses in recent years. Vandegrift, Vista Ridge, Leander, and Cedar Park High Schools are the district's campuses.

🏫 Georgetown ISD

Georgetown ISD serves the city of Georgetown and has experienced its own growth surge as the city has expanded rapidly. The district has been building new campuses to accommodate a fast-growing student population and has invested in modernizing existing facilities. Georgetown High School and East View High School serve the secondary population. The district's "Recognized" accountability ratings reflect solid performance with room for continued improvement as resources expand.

🏫 Hutto ISD

Hutto ISD serves Hutto and portions of eastern Williamson County. The district has grown significantly with Hutto's development and has been building new facilities to match. The Hutto Hippos athletic program has developed a strong community following. Academic performance has been improving steadily as the district has invested in staff and curriculum.

What Matters When Evaluating Schools

Specific campus performance: District-level ratings mask significant campus-to-campus variation within the same district. Look up the specific elementary, middle, and high school assigned to any home you're considering at the Texas Education Agency website.

Boundary changes: Fast-growing districts like LISD and GISD frequently redraw boundaries as new campuses open. Confirm that boundary changes aren't planned before assuming a home's school assignment is stable.

Program offerings: Dual-language programs, STEM academies, fine arts programs, and career and technical education offerings vary significantly across campuses within the same district.

💡 School District Research Tips
  • Texas Education Agency: txschools.gov has campus-specific ratings, demographic data, and performance data for every school in the state. This is the authoritative source.
  • Boundary lookup: Each district's website has a boundary lookup tool — enter the address of a home you're considering to confirm the specific campus assignments.
  • School tour timing: Contact the specific schools you're considering to arrange a campus visit in January-March before making a purchase decision. Most schools accommodate prospective family visits.
