Best Moving Companies in Central Texas: What to Know Before You Move

📅 April 2026  ·  CentralTexas.online Editorial

Moving is one of the highest-stakes service purchases most people make — you're trusting strangers with everything you own, often on a non-negotiable schedule, under stress. Central Texas's active real estate market means thousands of families move here monthly. Here's the complete guide to getting your move right.

Choosing a Moving Company: The Non-Negotiable Criteria

Licensing and Insurance

Any legitimate Texas moving company must be registered with the Texas Department of Motor Vehicles (TxDMV). You can verify any mover's license at txdmv.gov. Movers operating without a TxDMV license have no accountability when things go wrong — and things do go wrong. Ask for the license number before getting a quote and verify it. Also confirm they carry cargo insurance that covers the full replacement value of your belongings.

Written Binding Estimates

Never hire a moving company that won't provide a written, binding estimate. A non-binding estimate is a number that can change on moving day — sometimes dramatically. Binding estimates lock the price based on the agreed scope. Any legitimate company provides binding estimates; any company that refuses should be eliminated from consideration.

In-Home Surveys for Large Moves

For moves with more than 1,000 sq ft of household goods, insist on an in-home survey before the estimate. Estimates based on phone conversations are unreliable for full household moves. A company that is willing to estimate a full house move over the phone without seeing it is either inexperienced or planning to adjust the price on moving day.

The Moving Timeline for Central Texas

  • 8-12 weeks out: Get 3 in-home binding estimates. Check TxDMV licenses. Reserve your date — summer moves (May-August) book 6-8 weeks in advance in Central Texas.
  • 4-6 weeks: Begin decluttering. Donate or sell items you won't move. Moving less is always the right answer.
  • 2-3 weeks: Order packing supplies. Begin packing non-essential items. Notify utilities, mail, subscriptions.
  • 1 week: Confirm all details with movers in writing. Pack daily-use items last.
  • Moving day: Do a walkthrough with the crew chief before they start loading. Do another walkthrough after loading to catch anything left behind. Inspect items as they're unloaded at the destination.
💡 Moving in Central Texas: Specific Tips
  • Summer heat: Central Texas summer (June-August) moves are genuinely physically demanding for movers. Start as early as possible — first truck at 7am is the ideal. Provide water and Gatorade. Tip generously for summer moves.
  • Piano moving: Never allow a general mover to move a piano without verifying they have proper equipment and experience. Piano damage from improper moving is devastating and common. White Glove Movers or a piano-specific company is worth the premium.
  • Valuation coverage: Check your homeowners insurance policy before paying for the mover's valuation coverage — your policy may already cover items in transit. If not, the mover's full-value protection (not basic released-value) is worth adding for moves involving high-value items.
