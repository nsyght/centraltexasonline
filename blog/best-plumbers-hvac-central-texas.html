Best Plumbers & HVAC Companies in Central Texas: Who to Call

📅 April 2026  ·  CentralTexas.online Editorial

Plumbing and HVAC emergencies are two of the most stressful home situations Central Texas homeowners face — and the stakes for choosing the wrong provider are high. Here's the guide to finding trusted providers and knowing what to ask before the emergency happens.

Central Texas-Specific Plumbing Issues

Central Texas has specific plumbing conditions that homeowners new to the region need to understand:

Post-tension slab foundations: Most Central Texas homes built after 1980 use post-tension concrete slab construction. Plumbing runs under the slab, and repairs require tunneling under the house — a significantly more expensive process than above-ground plumbing repair. When you have a slab leak, the diagnostic and repair process is different from what plumbers in other regions typically handle. Choose a plumber with specific post-tension slab experience.

Hard water: Central Texas's water supply is notably hard — high mineral content that accelerates water heater sediment buildup, damages fixtures, and reduces soap effectiveness. Water softeners extend appliance life significantly and are worth the investment in most Central Texas homes.

Tree root infiltration: The live oak and cedar tree density in established Central Texas neighborhoods means tree root infiltration of sewer lines is common. Camera inspection of sewer lines at home purchase is a meaningful precaution.

Central Texas-Specific HVAC Issues

Sizing matters enormously: An undersized AC system runs continuously during Central Texas summers without achieving the thermostat setpoint. An oversized system cycles on and off too quickly, leaving humidity issues. If your system is new but underperforming, sizing and ductwork may be the issue rather than the equipment.

Preventive maintenance pays: A spring AC tune-up (March) before the summer heat reveals refrigerant charge issues, coil cleanliness problems, and the minor issues that become major failures at 107°F in August. The tune-up cost is typically $80-150. An August emergency service call starts at significantly more and may require waiting 3-7 days for a technician during peak demand.

💡 Plumbing & HVAC Tips
  • Know your main shutoff: Every Central Texas homeowner should know where their main water shutoff valve is located before a pipe bursts. Walk through your home with your spouse or partner and locate it together. A burst pipe while you're searching for the shutoff valve adds significant water damage.
  • Water heater timing: Water heaters in Central Texas typically last 8-12 years. If yours is over 8 years old, budget for replacement and discuss the conventional vs. tankless decision with your plumber before the current unit fails and forces an emergency decision.
  • HVAC filter schedule: Replace AC filters every 30-60 days during peak cooling season (May-September). Clogged filters reduce efficiency and cause the coil to ice over — a common summer service call that filter replacement would have prevented.
