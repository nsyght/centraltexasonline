Moving to Central Texas? Here's What You Need to Know (2026)

Central Texas is one of America's top relocation destinations. Here's the honest, practical guide to what you need to know before and after you arrive.

Welcome to One of America's Fastest-Growing Regions

Central Texas has been one of the top relocation destinations in the United States for most of the past decade. The combination of no state income tax, relatively affordable housing (compared to coastal metros), a booming job market, warm weather, and genuine quality of life has made the Austin metro and surrounding communities a magnet for families, remote workers, and businesses alike.

If you're moving here — or thinking about it — this guide covers what you actually need to know. The practical stuff. Where to live, what to expect, and how to find the services that matter.

Choosing Where to Live

The Austin metro is sprawling, and each suburb has a distinct character. Here's the honest breakdown:

⭕ Round Rock

The largest Williamson County city and home to Dell Technologies. Round Rock has the best combination of infrastructure, schools (Round Rock ISD), amenities, and proximity to Austin. Downtown is reviving. Dell Diamond baseball adds genuine community character. Housing is more expensive than outer suburbs but more accessible than Austin proper. Explore Round Rock businesses.

🌱 Georgetown

One of the most beautiful downtowns in Texas. Williamson County seat. Georgetown is experiencing enormous growth but has preserved its historic character well. Sun City makes it a top retirement destination. Strong schools (Georgetown ISD and some Leander ISD). Explore Georgetown businesses.

🌲 Cedar Park

Consistently ranked as one of the best places to live in Texas. Leander ISD (one of the state's top districts). Beautiful parks system including Brushy Creek. Slightly more expensive than Pflugerville or Hutto but the infrastructure and schools justify it for many families. Explore Cedar Park businesses.

🌊 Pflugerville

The most affordable of the major Williamson/Travis County suburbs. Excellent value. Growing business corridor. Pflugerville ISD. Lake Pflugerville is a genuine gem. Good for families who want Austin proximity without the Austin price tag. Explore Pflugerville businesses.

⚡ Taylor

One to watch. Samsung's $17 billion semiconductor plant is transforming Taylor's economy. Historic downtown is charming. Housing is still relatively affordable. If you work in tech or manufacturing and want space, Taylor is worth serious consideration.

Things People Don't Tell You About Central Texas

  • Summers are genuinely hot. 100+ degree days from June through September are common. Your AC is critical infrastructure. Don't wait for it to break before servicing it.
  • Traffic on I-35 is bad. Really bad. If you can avoid it during rush hour (7-9am, 4:30-7pm), do. The 183A toll road and 130 toll road are worth it.
  • Cedar fever is real. Mountain cedar (Ashe juniper) releases massive pollen clouds from December through February. Allergy sufferers should plan accordingly.
  • The community is welcoming. Central Texas has an outsider reputation for being unfriendly to newcomers, but most neighborhoods and cities are genuinely welcoming. Show up to local events, shop local, and you'll find your people.
  • Round Rock Donuts is mandatory. Consider this a public service announcement. Get a glazed donut within your first week.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best city to live in Central Texas?

Round Rock, Georgetown, and Cedar Park consistently rank as the best cities in Central Texas for families, based on school quality, safety, amenities, and quality of life. Round Rock offers the best infrastructure. Georgetown has the most beautiful historic downtown. Cedar Park has the top-ranked Leander ISD schools.

Is Central Texas a good place to live?

Central Texas is one of the top-ranked regions in the United States for quality of life, job growth, and livability. The region offers no state income tax, relatively affordable housing compared to coastal metros, warm weather, a booming tech job market, and a strong sense of community in cities like Round Rock, Georgetown, and Cedar Park.

What is the cost of living in Central Texas?

Central Texas is more affordable than Austin proper and significantly less expensive than coastal metros like San Francisco or New York. Round Rock and Pflugerville offer the best value among established cities. Georgetown and Cedar Park are slightly more expensive due to top-ranked schools and amenities. Taylor offers the most affordable housing as the city grows around the Samsung semiconductor plant.

What should I know before moving to Central Texas?

Key things to know: Texas summers are extremely hot (100+ degrees June-September), so a well-maintained AC is essential. I-35 traffic between Austin and Round Rock is consistently bad during rush hour — use 183A or SH-130 instead. Cedar fever (mountain cedar pollen) affects allergy sufferers December through February. H-E-B grocery stores are beloved community institutions. Round Rock Donuts is mandatory.
