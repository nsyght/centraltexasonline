Red Flags to Watch For

  • Quotes that change dramatically after teardown. Legitimate shops explain why; others use teardown as a hostage situation.
  • Pressure to decide immediately. Good shops give you time to think and get a second opinion.
  • No ASE certification on display. ASE certification isn't required, but shops that have it tend to advertise it.
  • Very few reviews or suspiciously perfect ratings. Real shops have mixed reviews — the pattern matters more than the score.
💡 Local Tip

For Texas state inspections, you don't have to use a shop you don't know. Any certified inspection station can do the sticker. Save the unfamiliar shop for the inspection, not the repair.
