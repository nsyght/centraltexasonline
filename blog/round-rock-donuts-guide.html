Round Rock Donuts — Why It's a Texas Legend

January 8, 2026  ·  4 min read
← Back to Blog

There are landmarks, and then there are institutions. Round Rock Donuts falls firmly in the second category. For nearly a century — since 1926 — this Round Rock bakery has been making the same Texas-sized golden glazed donuts that have made it one of the most recognized food businesses in the entire state of Texas.

What Makes Them Special

The donuts are big — noticeably, deliberately big. The signature golden glazed is the size of a small plate, and it's light, airy, and perfectly glazed in a way that takes decades of practice to replicate. That's the point: Round Rock Donuts has had nearly a century to perfect it. The glaze, the texture, the size — it's all been dialed in through generations of bakers.

The Central Texas Pilgrimage

People drive significant distances for these donuts. If you've lived in Central Texas for any length of time, you've made the pilgrimage. Visitors passing through on I-35 make deliberate detours. Food media has covered Round Rock Donuts extensively. It's the kind of place that represents something bigger than just breakfast pastry — it's a piece of Texas food identity.

Practical Tips

Go early. On weekends especially, the most popular items sell out by late morning. The shop opens at 6am daily and closes at 6pm or when they sell out — whichever comes first. Cash and card are accepted. Parking is easy during the week and manageable on weekends. Located at 106 W Liberty Ave in Round Rock, it's a short detour off I-35.

After donuts, the Round Rock area has plenty more worth exploring — including three of Central Texas's top party rental companies: Sandi's Moonwalks, Jump Around Party Rentals, and Hop A Lot Moonwalks.
